320 Years Ago

On February 2nd, 1704, the first school open to African-Americans in New York City was established as the Ashmun Institute

220 Years Ago

The French colony Saint-Domingue gains independence as Haiti on January 1st 1804

160 Years Ago

Dr. Rebecca Lee Crumpler becomes the first African-American woman in the US to earn an MD in 1864

160 Years Ago

On April 8, 1864, the Thirteenth Amendment abolishing slavary in the US was passed by Senate

100 Years Ago

Shirley Anita Chisholm was born. Chisholm was an American politician who, in 1968, became the first black woman to be elected to the United States Congress.

90 Years Ago

Audre Lorde was born. Lorde was an American writer, professor, philosopher, intersectional feminist, poet and civil rights activist. She was a self-described "Black, lesbian, feminist, socialist, mother, warrior, poet" who dedicated her life and talents to confronting different forms of injustice.

80 Years Ago

At age 14, African-American teenage boy George Stinney Jr. becomes the youngest person ever executed by electric chair in the United States after being wrongfully convicted of murder.

80 Years Ago

Akasha Gloria Hull (born December 6, 1944) an American poet, educator, writer, and critic whose work in African-American literature and as a Black feminist activist has helped shape Women's Studies.

80 Years Ago

Angela Yvonne Davis is a scholar, author, activist, and black feminist philosopher. She is one of the most recognized political activists of the 1960s and 1970s. As an advocate for prisoners' rights, Davis is a founding member of Critical Resistance, a national organization dedicated to abolishing the prison industrial complex. She writes and lectures on social injustice, social movements, and the intersections of race, gender, and class. She has published many books, including Angela Davis: An Autobiography (1974)

80 Years Ago

Alice Malsenior Tallulah-Kate Walker (born February 9, 1944) is an American novelist, short story writer, poet, and social activist.

60 Years Ago

Supreme Court decides Brown v. Board of Education, arguing that "separate but equal" schools are inherently unequal. The decision legally prohibits school segregation, declaring it unconstitutional.

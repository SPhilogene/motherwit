10 Years Ago (2014)

Black-ish premieres on September 24th, 2014. The show addresses social issues, tackled emotional family problems such as death and divorce, and even got the audience to understand that growth is something that should happen within a family.

130 Years Ago (1894)

Bessie Smith is born on April 15, 1894. Smith was an African-American blues singer widely renowned during the Jazz Age. Nicknamed the "Empress of the Blues", she was the most popular female blues singer of the 1930s.

100 Years Ago (1924)

The Harlem Renaissance begins in 1924. This marked the beginning of an intellectual and cultural revival of African-American music, dance, art, fashion, literature, theater, politics and scholarship centered in Harlem, spanning the 1920s and 1930s.

100 Years Ago (1924)

Ella Jenkins, born August 6, 1924, is now 100! She is an American singer-songwriter and has been called "The First Lady of the Children's Folk Song." She has been a leading performer of folk and children's music.

90 Years Ago (1934)

Zora Neale Hurston publishes her first novel, Jonah's Gourd Vine (1934), a semi-autobiographical account of her parents' lives. This is the first of over 50 works written by Hurston, including Their Eyes Were Watching God (1937).

100 Years Ago (1924)

Sonia Sanchez, a poet, activist, playwright, and teacher, is born on September 9, 1934. Sanchez was a leading figure in the Black Arts Movement and has written over 30 books including A Blues Book for Blue Black Magical Women (1974) and homegirls & handgrenades (1984).

50 Years Ago (1974)

Good Times debuts on February 8, 1974. The show was a television first for its portrayal of a two-parent African American family living in an inner-city housing project, showing the daily struggles and triumphs of the working-class.

50 Years Ago (1974)

The movie The Autobiography of Miss Jane Pittman starring Cicely Tyson premieres on January 31, 1974. Tyson, born in 1924, was known for her groundbreaking portrayals of strong, determined, elegant and dignified Black women.

40 Years Ago (1984)

Octavia Butler publishes Clay's Ark in 1984. This is the last of Patternist series, a five novel collection she began working on in 1974, which explores themes of race, power dynamics, and the complexities of human nature.

20 Years Ago (2004)

The L Word starring Jennifer Beales premieres on January 18, 2004. The L Word featured television's first ensemble cast of lesbian, bisexual, and queer women. It was also the first television series written and directed by predominantly queer women.
